Golden Visa

In order to obtain Portugal’s Golden Visa, there were some requirements that needed to be met to ensure a successful application, including:

No previous convictions on a criminal record
A non-EU or non-Swiss citizen
Be at least 18 years old
Choose the option of investing

Once the application was successful, investors were granted a Golden Visa for two years. In order to renew the Visa for the next three years, thus completing the required period, they had to visit Portugal for 14 days each year.

Funds of risk

Investments in funds investing in commercial organizations are more profitable, but also more risky, experienced investors know this. Only proven management companies participate in the Golden Visa program, and startups cannot participate.

The minimum investment amount here is 500,000€, the deposit can be returned after 5 years if you apply for citizenship, but it must be left in the fund to continue to maintain residency status

D7 Visa -
Portugal Retirement Visa or Passive Income Visa

The Portuguese government introduced a special type of visa in 2007: the D7 visa.  It is also known as the Retirement Visa or Passive Income Visa.

This residence visa allows any non-EU/EEA/Swiss citizen who has a reasonable regular net passive income to apply for residence in Portugal. This income can come from pensions, transferable equity, real estate, intellectual property, or financial investment.

The minimum amount required to be eligible for this visa is an annual income of 8,460 euros, plus 50% of this amount for the spouse and 30% for each dependent child. This is regardless of the person's country of origin.

This visa allows you and your family to reside legally in Portugal. It is designed to attract retired people and those living on passive income to reside in Portugal, in an effort to boost the economy. In addition, holders of this visa are not prevented from working in Portugal.
